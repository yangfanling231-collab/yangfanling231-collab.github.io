George Russell

George Russell, born in 1998 in the United Kingdom, is an established Formula One driver for Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS. Known for his calm and professional approach, he built his reputation with strong performances at Williams before joining Mercedes. In 2022, he secured his first Grand Prix victory with the team, proving his ability to compete at the highest level. As the more experienced driver in the lineup, Russell is valued for his consistency, qualifying strength, and leadership, playing a crucial role in delivering points and podiums for the team.

Andrea “Kimi” Antonelli

Andrea “Kimi” Antonelli, born in 2006 in Italy, is one of the youngest drivers to race in Formula One. A karting prodigy with multiple junior championship titles, he joined the Mercedes Junior Programme in 2019 and quickly rose through the ranks. In 2025, at just 18 years old, he made his F1 debut with Mercedes, showcasing impressive speed and maturity beyond his age. His standout performances, such as a top-four finish at the Australian Grand Prix, have highlighted his huge potential. As the team’s rising star, Antonelli brings fresh energy and ambition to complement Russell’s experience.










































Mercedes W16

The 2025 Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS F1 car is named the Mercedes-AMG F1 W16 E PERFORMANCE (commonly just “W16”). It’s the team’s challenger for the 2025 FIA Formula One World Championship.
























Chassis & Materials

The W16 uses a carbon fibre monocoque structure (as is standard in F1) with bodywork and aerodynamic surfaces also made from carbon composites. A key innovation for 2025 is that Mercedes is integrating sustainable carbon fibre composites — making parts of the car with eco-friendlier resin systems and fibre sources — in an effort to reduce the environmental footprint while maintaining performance and safety standards

Mercedes Achievements

Mercedes-Benz has a rich history in motorsports. It first entered Formula One in the 1950s, where its driver Juan Manuel Fangio won back-to-back Drivers’ Championships in 1954 and 1955, and Mercedes also secured Constructors’ titles in that period

Mercedes returned to F1 as an engine supplier in 1994, and later entered as a full constructor in 2010 by acquiring the Brawn GP team.

    Some additional notable achievements:

  • Under the partnership with Lewis Hamilton, Mercedes achieved huge success: together they collected many wins, podiums, and pole positions.
  • The Mercedes power unit (engine + hybrid system) has also been extremely successful: since 2014, Mercedes-powered cars have registered very high numbers of pole positions, wins, and championship points.
  • Mercedes has been recognized for technical excellence, receiving awards for innovation, e.g. prizes like the Dewar Trophy (for technical achievements) have been awarded multiple times.
